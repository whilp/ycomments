Y Comments

Get the code!

Do your blog posts end with exhortations to "Discuss this on Hacker News"? Do you like plugging into the engaged and clueful HN community but worry about sending your readers off to some site that's not your own? Do you wish it was all a little bit more like Disqus? Well then.

This here is a bit of Javascript that gives you the best of both worlds: it dumps HN comment threads below your article, just like Disqus (sort of). To do this, Y Comments relies on the third-party HackerNews API to extract the comment threads from the Hacker News post about your article.

This is handy for static blogs or for authors that want to tap into the clueful and engaged HN community. With Y Comments, you can have it all.

To get started, write something interesting and post it on Hacker News. Then, add the following at the bottom of your article: 




]]>

Now, when people read your article and start commenting on it at HN, their comments will show up under your article. When you find bugs, fork the code, fix them and send a pull request. It's all BSD licensed, you know.

Some things to consider:

  • Compatibility with document types that aren't XHTML5 and browsers that aren't Firefox 3.6.1.3/OpenBSD is unknown.
    • That said, it should be pretty easy to extend the hack to output HTML that works with your documents; see for example $.fn.ycomments.thread().
  • Features like votes and replies haven't actually been tested yet.
  • This hack is awfully accepting of whatever it gets from HN and the HN API.
  • This hack is woefully understyled; improvements to its CSS will be gladly accepted.
  • This hack may not make idiomatic use of Javascript and jQuery; my apologies.

This hack was created at and for the 2011 Build Madison 24-hour hackathon by Will Maier. It did not take 24 hours.
